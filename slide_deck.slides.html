What affect Borrower's Interest Rate?¶

by Anh Tran Thi Ngoc¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I wanted to look at the fetures that could be used to predict the interest rate of borrowers at Prosper. The main focus was on Prosper Score, Average Credit Rating, Employemnt Status and Listing Category.

Dataset Overview¶

The data contains 113,937 loans with 81 variables on each loan, including loan amount, borrower rate (or interest rate), current loan status, borrower income, and many others. However, because of missing data issues, only data after July 2009 is interpreted.

Distribution of Borrower's Interest Rate¶

Borrowers who request a loan at Prosper have to pay interest rate from 5% to 35%. The distribution of interest rate looks multimodal, with the highest peak at 0.32. This peak is nearly double the second peak, which is around 0.15.

Distribution of Prosper Score¶

Proper Score is a custom risk score built using historical Prosper data. The score ranges from 1-10, with 10 being the best, or lowest risk score. The majority of loan has a custom risk score around 4 to 8. Thus, it can be said that the risk level of the loan is from fairly risky to medium.

Distribution of Prosper Rating¶

Prosper encodes their rating as follow: 1 - HR, 2 - E, 3 - D, 4 - C, 5 - B, 6 - A, 7 - AA, in which HR stands for high risk customer and AA is a customer with high credit quality. More than 20% of borrowers at Prosper have a credit grade of C. Majority of loan at Prosper is for customers who have the credit grade of D, C, B and A. Although Prosper still provides credit for high risk customer, the proportion of them is quite low.

Interest Rate vs Prosper Rating, Employment Status and Home Ownership¶

It appears to have a negative relationship between Proper rating and interest rate. The loan with a higher rating or with higher credit quality will have a lower interest rate. Addionally, employed borrowers has the lowest mean of interest. Borrowers who are unemployed tend to have a higher interest rate. The innerquartile range of interest of full-time and part-time borrowers is larger than others. It seems that different listing category will have different average and innerquartile interest rate. Borrowers who own a home tend to have lower interest rate on average

Interest Rate vs Average Credit Score and Prosper Score¶

People who have an low average credit score, which is between 600 and 630, must pay highest rate. Most of them have to pay interest rate between 20% to 35%. On the other hand, most of high credit quality borrowers who have average credit score higher than 830 only pay interest rate below 15%. For medium risk borrowers, the rating score seems to have no strong relationship with the interest rate that they have to pay. We can see the same pattern with Prosper Score. The difference in interest rate is only clear if the borrowers are high risk or high credit quality.

Interest Rate vs Imployment Status and Home Ownership¶

When borrowers own a home, the lender tends to evaluate them less risky than others because of their higher capacity to repay the loan. Thus, borrowers who are homeowner appear to have lower interest rate when they make a loan. However, in our graph, it is only true with employed, full-time and self-employed borrowers. For unemployed borrowers, they pay higher interest when they own a home. There is no clear difference in interest rate for part-time borrowers whether they own a home or not.
